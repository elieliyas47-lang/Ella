 <h2 style="text-align:center;fonte-size:6rem">1-HARDTACK</h2>
      <p> A simple, unleavened biscuit made from flour, water, and occasionally salt. It is baked multiple times at low temperatures to remove all moisture, resulting in a cracker that is literally "hard as a tack."</p>
    <ul>
      Advantage of hardtack 
      <li>​Immortal Shelf-Life: Properly stored, it can last for years (or decades) without spoiling.</li>
        <li>​Caloric Density: Provides a concentrated source of carbohydrates for energy.</li>
      <li>​Durability: It won't crush or crumble easily in a pack or a ship’s hold.</li>
      <li>Filler: When soaked in liquid, it expands, helping you feel full on small rations.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div  style="background:linear-gradient(to left,  blue,black);color:white;border-style:double;border-radius:50px;boder-width:30px;margin:40px auto;padding:50px">
    <h2 style="text-align:center;fonte-size:6rem">2-COCONUT</h2>
    <p> The fruit of the coconut palm (Cocos nucifera). In a survival context, it is often referred to as the "Tree of Life" because every part of it is usable.</p> 
    <ul>
      Advantage of coconut
      <li>Hydration: The "water" inside is full of electrolytes (potassium and magnesium).</li>
      <li>​High Fat Content: The white meat provides essential fats and calories that hardtack lacks.</li>
​<li>Utility: The shell can be used as a bowl or fuel, and the husk (coir) makes excellent tinder for fires.</li>
      <li>​Sterile: Until cracked, the water inside is generally free of bacteria.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div style="background:linear-gradient(to right, cyan,tomato);color:white;border-style:double;border-radius:50px;boder-width:30px;margin:40px auto;padding:50px;">
    <h2 style="text-align:center;fonte-size:6rem">3- PLUNDERED FRUITS</h2>
    <p> Fresh produce (typically tropical like mangoes, limes, or pineapples) "acquired" from islands or intercepted ships during a journey.</p>
    <ul>
      Advantage of plundered fruits
      <li>​Scurvy Prevention: Fresh fruit provides Vitamin C, which is the only thing keeping your teeth from falling out on a long voyage.</li>
      <li>​Morale Boost: After weeks of dry biscuits, the sugar and juice of fresh fruit provide a massive psychological lift.</li>
      <li>​Digestive Health: Provides the fiber necessary to offset a heavy diet of hardtack.</li>
    </ul>
